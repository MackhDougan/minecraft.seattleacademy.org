Vanderbilt

The Vanderbilt is the oldest building SAAS owns and it has only 2 floors. The heating is always on too high, and the ceilings are leaky. SAAS is planing on remodeling it soon. The Vanderbilt is one of my favorite buildings as it's got lots of winding hallways and if you know your way around you can find a nice quiet place with very few, if any people.

Middle school

The middle school building is SAAS's newest building. It's five stories tall, and has it's own gym as well as a rooftop playfeild. Each grade has a floor to themselves. It's also much harder to get lost in this building than older buildings, which also means less hidey holes and quiet spots.

The Gym

The Gym is conected to the arts center and often mistaken as part of it, it's also connected to the stream building but the two buildings are so differant not nearly as many people get them confused. As a very unathletic person I pretty much only use the gym as a short cut to get somewhere, but I do know that the gym building contains bathrooms, locker rooms, a mezzanine with a bunch of equipment that students can use any time, a work out room, as well as one of the dance studios.

Stream

The stream is SAAS's second newest building, and so has very similar arcitechure to the middle school. It contains the science classes for all of high school. The stairs get very annoying very quickly, and given how symmetrical it is I'd recomend keeping a close eye on floor and room number so you don't get lost, and if you need a quiet spot during lunch the fifth floor is often very empty.

Arts center

The arts center is an older building but it's still in good shape. Both math and most arts classes happen here. This building contains two theaters, the main theater and the black box. Both middle school and high school arts classes take place here.

Temple

The temple is a block away from all SAAS's other buildings and it's very old, leaking ceilings and bad heating galore. The temple is used for high school humanities classes and a few study skills classes. One of the water fountains has been broken for 4 years now.
